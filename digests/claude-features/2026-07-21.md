# Claude + AI Digest — 2026-07-21

**[New capability]** Claude Code's changelog (v2.1.215–2.1.216) shows two behavior changes: Claude no longer auto-runs the `/verify` and `/code-review` skills on its own — you now have to invoke them explicitly — and a new `sandbox.filesystem.disabled` setting lets you skip filesystem isolation while still keeping network egress control. Source: official CHANGELOG.md on the claude-code GitHub repo (no exact date on entries, but these are the newest two). Tie-in: if you were relying on auto-review firing at the end of a session (e.g. inside Council Loop's Realist step or a NEXUS coding pass), that safety net is gone until you explicitly call `/code-review` or `/verify` — worth a quick check that nothing in your loop config assumed the old auto-fire behavior.

**[New technique]** Claude Code has session "teleportation": `claude --cloud "<prompt>"` starts a session on claude.ai, `claude --teleport <session-id>` (or `/teleport`) pulls a cloud/mobile session down into your local terminal with full history and the right branch checked out, and `/remote-control` lets your phone or claude.ai steer a session that's actually running on your machine. Confirmed via Anthropic's Boris Cherny (Claude Code lead) directly and the official remote-control docs, not just secondhand blog coverage. Tie-in: you could kick off a long NEXUS or Council Loop task from your phone via Hermes-adjacent workflows, then teleport it into your terminal once you're back at your desk to finish debugging — or the reverse, start locally and steer a long-running session from your phone with `/remote-control` instead of checking in via SSH.

**[Claude buzz]** A rumor spread this week that a July 14 Claude update was silently deleting users' conversations; one explainer (origami.sa) traced it back to Anthropic's actual July 14 release notes (a HIPAA self-serve config change for orgs, unrelated to chat storage) and attributes the vanishing-chat reports to a client-side crash-before-save bug instead. I could only verify this via a single explainer piece, not primary reach/engagement data, so treat "how big this got" as unconfirmed — flagging it more as a live rumor-to-watch than confirmed viral buzz.

**[Other AI]** Moonshot AI (China) released Kimi K3 on July 16 — a 2.8-trillion-parameter model billed as the largest open-source/open-weight release to date, with a 1M-token context window and native visual understanding. It landed #3 on the Artificial Analysis leaderboard (behind Claude Fable 5 and GPT-5.6 Sol) but topped a front-end coding benchmark, and demand has been strong enough that Moonshot paused new subscriptions on July 20 to manage compute load; full weights are due by July 27. Reported across Bloomberg, VentureBeat, Fortune, and CoinDesk. Tie-in: once weights land July 27, it's a candidate to add to your OpenRouter pipeline as a cheap-but-strong option for front-end-heavy generation steps.
